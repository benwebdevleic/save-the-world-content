Charity shops help charities, the environment and the community in many ways:

  • Fundraising: every year, charity shops raise more than Â£295m (data from the Charity Retail Association), funding causes such as medical research, overseas aid, hospice care, homeless shelters, environmental initiatives and animal welfare projects, among many others.
  • Brand awareness: a presence on the high street increases awareness of the parent charity and its work
  • Environmental benefits: selling second hand goods prevents them going to landfill. All clothing that cannot be sold is recycled, for example. In the financial year 2018/19, shops removed 339,000 tonnes of textiles from the waste stream (data from the Charity Retail Association)
  • Budget shopping: charity shops are a dependable source of low cost but good quality items
  • Ethical benefits new bought-in goods are often Fairtrade products, while reusing goods prevents outsourced sweatshop labour
  • Volunteering opportunities volunteers in stores can make new friends and learn new skills as they gain work experience
